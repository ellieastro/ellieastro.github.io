Current Research

My PhD research primarily focuses on the observational analysis of the multiple stellar populations present within 30 Galactic Globular Clusters (GCs). To gain a wide-field view of each GC, I employed a two-fold approach. Firstly, I used Hubble Space Telescope photometry to target the central regions of the cluster. Secondly, I used ground-based photometry from the extensive catalogue provided by Stetson et al. (2019) to observe the outer regions. Using both sets of photometric data, I separated the stars into their respective multiple populations and determined their spatial distributions within the cluster.

This investigation sought to determine whether any of the stellar populations exhibited central concentration. The homogeneous analysis of this large sample of diverse GCs revealed a wide range of spatial distributions, further advancing our understanding of the possible formation scenarios of GCs and the mysterious multiple stellar populations they contain. For more information on this section of my PhD project, see my recent paper.
